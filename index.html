The people behind it
About

Walark Electronics

Walark Electronics develops embedded products and educational hardware from Christchurch, New Zealand. The E4 Educator board was designed to support a curriculum that teaches embedded systems the way they are built professionally — not as a toy, but as a discipline.

Fundrum Engineering

Fundrum Engineering is the firmware and product engineering practice of Mark Weir — embedded systems developer and leading drummer with 56 years of competitive pipe band experience. Commercial products include DRUMLINE (snare tuner) and DRONELINE (bagpipe drone tuner), both built on ESP32.

Who this is for

Advanced hobbyists who have outgrown Arduino sketches and want to build real networked instruments. Teachers looking for a structured embedded systems curriculum with production-quality code from day one. Anyone who wants to understand what is actually happening inside a connected device.

The E4 board

The Enceladus Educator v2.0 is a socketed ESP32 carrier board with every peripheral needed for the full curriculum on a single PCB — ILI9341 TFT, XPT2046 touch, microSD, AHT20, ICS43434 MEMS mic, DS18B20 header, LDR, RGB LED, piezo, and DAC header. Available from Walark Electronics.
